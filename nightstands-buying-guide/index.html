How to choose a nightstands

Updated June 2026Independently researchedNo paid placement.

Quick answer

To choose a nightstand with a built-in charging station, focus on your bed height, the number of devices you charge nightly, and the type of ports you prefer (USB-A vs. USB-C). Look for a size that fits your bedside space without crowding, and pick a material (solid wood, MDF, or metal) that matches your decor and durability needs. Also consider cord management features like grommets or hidden compartments to keep wires tidy.

What size nightstand should I choose?

Measure your bed height first, your nightstand top should be roughly level with your mattress, usually between 25 and 30 inches from the floor. Most people choose a tabletop that is a few inches lower than the mattress so it’s easy to reach. For width, allow at least a few inches of clearance between the nightstand and your bed frame so you can slide it in and out without scraping. Depth matters too: a surface around 16 to 18 inches deep gives room for a lamp, phone, and a glass without feeling cramped, but narrower depths work better for tight spaces like a small guest room. If you have a low platform bed, you may want a shorter nightstand in the 20–24 inch range. For taller beds with a box spring, look for stands that reach the 28–32 inch mark. Always measure your bedside space including any door swings or drawer pulls on adjacent furniture.

How many charging ports do I actually need?

Think about the devices you plug in overnight: a phone, maybe a smartwatch, earbuds, or a tablet. One or two USB ports are fine for a single person, but couples sharing a nightstand often appreciate three or four ports, or a combination of USB-A and USB-C. If you also need to charge a laptop or an iPad, a model with a dedicated AC outlet is much more practical than relying on USB alone. Fast charging support is worth checking: many newer nightstands include USB-C Power Delivery (PD) that can charge a phone in under an hour. For older devices, standard 5V/2A USB-A ports still work well. Don’t buy a nightstand with only one low-power USB port if you plan to charge multiple gadgets simultaneously, it’ll charge slowly and cause frustration.

Are wireless charging pads on nightstands reliable?

Wireless charging pads built into nightstands are convenient, you just set your phone down and it starts charging. However, reliability depends on the pad’s power output (most common is 5W to 15W) and whether it supports your phone’s specific wireless standard. Qi-compatible pads work with iPhones (8 and later) and most Android phones, but you must place the phone precisely on the marked spot, often a mat or a raised area. Some users report that thick phone cases or cases with metal components can block charging. Also, the pad’s position on the nightstand matters: if it’s embedded in a drawer or under a wooden top that’s too thick, the charging range may be reduced. For the best experience, look for a model where the charging pad is clearly indicated and the surface is less than 5mm away from the coil.

What materials work best for a nightstand with charging?

Solid wood (oak, walnut, or birch) offers the best durability and a classic look, but it tends to be heavier and more expensive. Many charging nightstands use engineered wood like MDF or particleboard with a veneer finish, these are budget-friendly and lighter, but they can warp if exposed to moisture from a humidifier or a glass of water. Metal frames (steel or aluminum) are sturdy and modern but may feel cold and can scratch flooring if not equipped with felt pads. Consider the top surface: a smooth, flat finish is easier to clean and works best for wireless charging. Avoid glossy finishes if you plan to put a lamp or water on top, they show smudges and water rings quickly. For longevity, a matte or lightly textured surface is more forgiving.

How do I manage cable clutter on a charging nightstand?

Cable clutter is the biggest complaint with charging nightstands. Look for models that include a built-in wire management grommet, a small plastic or metal ring on the back of the unit that lets you feed power cords through. Some nightstands also have a small compartment or channel underneath the tabletop to hide excess cable length. Drawers with pass-through cutouts are another smart feature: you can run a charging cord from the interior drawer into the tabletop space. If the nightstand doesn’t include management features, you can add adhesive cable clips along the back leg or use a short extension cord with a flat plug that stays close to the wall. The goal is to avoid having thick power bricks dangling or a tangled knot of wires visible from the bed.

Should I choose a nightstand with a built-in power strip or USB ports?

Built-in power strips (with two or three AC outlets plus USB ports) are more versatile because you can plug in full-sized chargers for laptops, CPAP machines, or lighting. They usually connect to the wall via a permanently attached cord, which means you can’t easily replace the strip if it fails. USB-only models are simpler and often slimmer, but they limit you to charging devices that accept USB. no AC plugs. For most people, a combination is best: at least two AC outlets and two USB ports (preferably one USB-C with PD). This setup handles almost any bedside need. If you travel frequently, a removable power strip or USB hub (like a detachable module) is a plus because you can swap out the electronics if standards change. However, these models are less common and cost a bit more.

Frequently asked questions

Can I use any nightstand and add a charging station myself?

Yes, you can retrofit most nightstands with a USB charging station or power strip by drilling a hole for the cord or using stick-on modules. But built-in charging stations often have cleaner cable management and a more polished look. If you’re handy, a DIY solution can save money, but you lose the seamless integration and warranty of a dedicated model.

Do charging nightstands require special wiring or an electrician?

No, most charging nightstands plug directly into a standard wall outlet, just like a lamp. They don’t need hardwiring or an electrician. The built-in charging components are usually low-voltage and comply with safety regulations. However, if you want a nightstand with a hardwired power source (e.g., into a wall switch), that would require professional installation, but such models are rare.

Are there fire safety concerns with charging nightstands?

Any electronic device carries a small fire risk, but reputable charging nightstands should be certified by a safety lab like UL or ETL. Look for these marks on the product listing or label. Overheating is possible if the nightstand’s electrical components are poorly insulated or if you cover the charging pad with blankets. To be safe, never stack papers or fabrics on the charging area while it’s in use.

How much weight can a nightstand with charging hold?

Weight capacity depends on the construction of the nightstand, not the charging feature. A typical MDF or particleboard nightstand can hold 30 to 50 pounds across its surface, while solid wood or metal models can often support 60 to 80 pounds. Always check the manufacturer’s recommended maximum load, especially if you plan to place a large lamp or a TV on top.

Can I get a nightstand with both USB-A and USB-C ports?

Yes, many newer models include both types. USB-C ports are increasingly common, especially those that support Power Delivery (PD) for rapid charging of modern smartphones and tablets. USB-A ports are still useful for older cables and accessories. A combination of two USB-A and one USB-C is a good balance for most households.

Are there nightstands with hidden charging compartments?

Yes, some high-end nightstands offer a small drawer or lift-top section where you can place your phone to charge wirelessly, hiding it from view. This is a neat solution for keeping the top surface clutter-free, but it often means your phone is out of sight and may block wireless signals or prevent you from seeing notifications easily. They also tend to cost more than open charging pads.

In shortChoosing the right nightstand with a charging station comes down to matching the size, port types, and material to your daily routine. Measure your space, count the devices you charge each night, and prioritise cord management features that suit your habits. A well-chosen nightstand keeps your sleep area organised and your devices ready to go, without the morning scramble for an outlet.
